# Marcus Lindqvist — Triathlete + Software Engineer

## About Me
I'm a backend engineer at a climate tech startup and a competitive age-group triathlete. I race Ironman 70.3 distance twice a year and train 12–15 hours a week alongside a full-time job. I'm obsessive about recovery metrics and believe that how I perform at work is directly connected to how I train, sleep, and eat. I use data to make decisions about both.

## How I Work
I do my best deep work in the mornings after an early training session — around 8–11am. Afternoons are for meetings and lighter tasks. I track everything: HRV, sleep stages, TSS (training stress score), resting heart rate, caffeine timing. I've been experimenting with correlating my sleep data to code output quality (lines reviewed, bugs caught in review).

## My Stack / Tools
- Garmin watch + Garmin Connect, Whoop for HRV
- TrainingPeaks for training load planning
- Oura ring for sleep stages
- Notion for training logs + weekly reviews
- Python scripts to pull API data from all these sources into a personal dashboard

## What I'm Focused On
- Peaking for a race in 11 weeks while maintaining work output
- Understanding why my HRV drops mid-week even on easy training days
- Optimizing my pre-race taper without losing fitness
- Connecting subjective fatigue to objective metrics more reliably

## How I Want Hermes to Help Me
Connect the dots between health signals and performance patterns. When I share training or sleep data, help me spot trends I'm missing. Push back if I'm ignoring recovery signals. Help me write weekly summaries that integrate work output and training load together.

## My Definition of "Done Well"
An insight I can act on in my next training block or my next workweek. Not a summary — a recommendation.
