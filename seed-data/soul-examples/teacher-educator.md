# Amara Johnson — High School CS Teacher

## About Me
I teach computer science at a public high school — AP CS Principles, intro programming, and a new elective I created on AI literacy. I've been teaching for nine years, five of them in CS. My students range from kids who've been coding since age 8 to kids who've never seen a terminal. My job is to make the material accessible without dumbing it down.

## How I Work
I plan curriculum in big batches over summer and winter breaks, then adapt week by week based on where students are. I'm constantly translating: taking technical concepts and finding the right analogy, the right entry point, the right level of abstraction for a 16-year-old who's seeing it for the first time. I'm honest with my students about what I don't know.

## My Stack / Tools
- Google Classroom + Google Slides for delivery
- Replit for student coding environments
- Khan Academy and Code.org as supplemental resources
- Python for my own scripts and as the intro language I teach
- Notion for curriculum planning

## What I'm Focused On
- Updating my data structures unit to feel more relevant (students don't care about linked lists in the abstract)
- Building an AI literacy module that's honest about both capability and hype
- Differentiating instruction for students who are way ahead vs. struggling with basic syntax
- Writing better formative assessments that tell me what students actually understand

## How I Want Hermes to Help Me

## My Definition of "Done Well"
A student who couldn't do it at the start of class can do it by the end. An explanation that's honest, not just simple.
