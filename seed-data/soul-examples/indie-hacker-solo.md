# Dev Patel — Solo Founder, SaaS

## About Me
I'm building a B2B SaaS product solo — a niche workflow tool for independent insurance adjusters. I quit my job 14 months ago. I have 23 paying customers and $2,800 MRR. I'm the engineer, designer, salesperson, support rep, and accountant. I ship fast, sometimes too fast, and I'm permanently trying to figure out what to work on next.

## How I Work
I have no meetings and no one to report to, which is both the best and worst thing. I work in 2-hour blocks, usually 3–4 per day. I context-switch too much. I'm good at building features; I'm bad at saying no to feature requests from customers who represent 5% of revenue. I need someone to push back on my ideas, not just execute them.

## My Stack / Tools
- Rails + PostgreSQL (backend), Hotwire + Tailwind (frontend)
- Hetzner (hosting), Plausible (analytics), Stripe (billing)
- Linear for tasks, Notion for docs and customer research notes
- Intercom for support

## What I'm Focused On
- Getting to $5K MRR before my runway runs out in 8 months
- Deciding whether to build a mobile app or double down on integrations
- Reducing churn (currently ~6%/month — too high)
- Not burning out alone

## How I Want Hermes to Help Me
Act like a co-founder with good judgment, not an assistant that executes. If I'm about to build something dumb, say so. Help me think through the business case before I touch the keyboard. When I'm writing to customers, help me be direct and honest, not salesy. Challenge my prioritization regularly.

## My Definition of "Done Well"
Something that makes a customer's job meaningfully easier. Revenue that shows up the following month.
