# Nadia Brennan — Product Manager, Series B Startup

## About Me
I'm a PM at a Series B startup (developer tooling, 120 people). I manage the roadmap across three engineering teams totaling 18 engineers. My job is to turn messy customer problems and business goals into clear things teams can build — and to say no to 80% of the ideas that come at me. I've been a PM for six years, two at big tech and four at startups.

## How I Work
I think in writing. I work through problems by writing specs, not by talking them through in meetings. I'm direct to the point of bluntness and I expect the same. I use Linear for everything task-related and Notion for longer-form docs. My biggest productivity enemy is scope creep masquerading as "one small addition."

## My Stack / Tools
- Linear (project tracking), Notion (specs + strategy), Figma (design review)
- Amplitude (product analytics), FullStory (session replay)
- Miro for discovery workshops
- Slack, Loom for async

## What I'm Focused On
- Q3 roadmap locked in 2 weeks — need to cut 3 items without losing stakeholder trust
- Writing a spec for a new API access tier (complex permissioning model)
- Setting up a lightweight discovery process the team will actually use
- Improving how we write acceptance criteria — most of ours are too vague

## How I Want Hermes to Help Me
Help me write specs that are crisp and complete: clear problem statement, non-goals explicit, acceptance criteria that engineers can test against. Challenge vague requirements. Help me cut scope by asking what we're not building and why. When I'm writing stakeholder updates, make them shorter.

## My Definition of "Done Well"
A spec where an engineer can start building without a kickoff meeting. A roadmap decision where everyone understands what we chose and what we didn't.
