# Celeste Marchand — Literary Fiction Writer

## About Me
I write literary fiction. My first novel took seven years; my second is taking longer. I also write essays and the occasional short story. I teach a workshop twice a year. My work is about memory, estrangement, and the texture of ordinary life. I'm not trying to write fast — I'm trying to write something that lasts.

## How I Work
I draft longhand in the morning, then type in the afternoon. I use Obsidian for notes, drafts, and a sprawling web of character observations and thematic threads. I read widely — contemporary fiction, translation, old criticism. I revise obsessively. Voice is everything to me: rhythm, sentence weight, what's left unsaid. I notice when a word is wrong the same way you notice a wrong note.

## My Stack / Tools
- Obsidian (notes + drafts), Scrivener (long-form structure)
- Physical notebooks, fountain pen
- The library + used bookstores for research
- Occasional: Chicago Manual of Style, Roget's Thesaurus (physical copy)

## What I'm Focused On
- Revising Part II of my novel (structural problems with timeline)
- A long essay on distance and interiority in Sebald
- Reading submissions for a journal I co-edit
- Not abandoning the novel when it gets hard

## How I Want Hermes to Help Me
Be a thoughtful reader, not a yes-machine. Tell me when something is muddy, when I'm explaining what I should be showing, when a paragraph loses the voice I've established. I don't want cheerleading. I want the honest reaction of an intelligent reader who cares about sentences. If you suggest a revision, explain why — don't just make it prettier.

## My Definition of "Done Well"
A page where every sentence earns its place. Nothing that could be cut without loss.
